Honda

Honda offers 5 car models in India, including 2 cars in Sedan category, 1 car in Hatchback category, 1 car in Compact Sedan category, 1 car in Compact SUV category. Honda has 2 upcoming cars in India, HR-V and New Jazz.


Honda Cars India (HCIL) is a subsidiary of Japanese automobile manufacturer Honda. Founded in 1995, the company has its headquarters located in Greater Noida, Uttar Pradesh. The first model to roll off the companyâ€™s production line in India was the City. The model has been on sale in the country for more than 20 years.


Currently, the product portfolio of Honda Cars India includes Amaze, BR-V, Jazz, WR-V, City, Civic, CR-V, and Accord hybrid. The City has been the most successful model for the brand in India. HCIL currently produces the aforementioned cars in two of its factories that are located at Greater Noida in Uttar Pradesh and Tapukara in Rajasthan.


For the first 15 years, Honda Cars India offered its products exclusively with petrol engines. In 2013, Honda launched the Amaze compact sedan, which was the first model from the company to be offered with a diesel engine. Currently, the iDTEC engine is offered on all Honda products except the Accord, which is a petrol-hybrid.

Honda Amaze

Honda Cars India has launched the facelifted Amaze in the country. The model is available in three variants including E, S, and VX, across five colour options such as Platinum White Pearl, Lunar Silver Metallic, Golden Brown Metallic, Radiant Red, and a new Meteroid Grey Metallic colour.

Honda all-new City

The all-new fifth-generation Honda City was launched in India in July 2020. The car is available in nine various trim levels. It comes with one petrol engine paired to either a six-speed manual or a seven-speed CVT automatic while the diesel engine is coupled with only a six-speed manual gearbox.

Honda Jazz

The Honda Jazz is the company's sole hatchback offering in India, competing agaist the likes of the Maruti Suzuki Baleno, Ford Figo, Hyundai i20 and others. The Jazz is a testament to Honda's ability to extract maximum interior room in a finite space.

Honda WR-5

The WR-V was first showcased in the Indian market in 2017. Honda later updated the car to meet BS6 emission norms in 2020 and it then also came with some added features. This car is available in six exterior paints - Platinum White Pearl, Radiant Red Metallic, Golden Brown Metallic, Lunar Silver Metallic, Premium Amber Metallic and Modern Steel Metallic.
