Hyundai

Hyundai car price starts at Rs 4.77 Lakh for the cheapest model which is Santro and the price of most expensive model, which is Kona Electric starts at Rs 23.79 Lakh. Hyundai offers 12 car models in India, including 4 cars in SUV category, 2 cars in Sedan category, 4 cars in Hatchback category, 1 car in Compact Sedan category, 1 car in Compact SUV category. Hyundai has 3 upcoming cars in India, New Kona, Casper and New Elantra.


Hyundai India is a fully owned subsidiary of South Korean automobile manufacturer, Hyundai. The Indian arm of the brand was founded in 1996 and has its headquarters located in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. The company launched its first model in the country, the Santro, in 1998.


Hyundai India has two production facilities located in Irangattukotai and Sriperumbudur in Tamil Nadu. Both the aforementioned plants have a combined production capacity of 7 lakh units per year. The company also uses these plants to exports models across the world.


Currently, Hyundai’s Indian product portfolio includes the second generation Santro, Grand i10, Xcent, Elite i20, i20 Active, Verna, Venue, Creta, Elantra and the Tucson. The company also launched its first fully electric vehicle in India, the Kona Electric.

Hyundai Venue

Hyundai entered the sub-four metre SUV segment with the debut of the Venue back in May 2019. The model is currently available with three engine options across seven variants that include E, S, S(O), S+, SX, EX(O) Exe, and SX (O).

6.99 Lakh

Hyundai Creta

The Hyundai Creta has now entered its second generation and was first showcased at the 2020 Auto Expo. It’s the king of the D-segment and is offered in 14 variants across three engine and gearbox options.

10.15 Lakh

Hyundai i20

The Hyundai i20 gets three engine options such as the 1.2-litre NA petrol engine, the 1.5-litre diesel engine, and the 1.0-ltire turbo-petrol engine. The latter produces 118bhp and 172Nm of torque, and is offered with a six-speed iMT unit and a seven-speed DCT unit.

6.91 Lakh

Hyundai Grand i10 Nios

The third-generation Grand i10 Nios is available in five different variants and is offered in three engine options. Moreover, it comes with five single-tone and three dual-tone exterior paint choices with an all-black roof.

5.28 lakh

Hyundai Aura

Hyundai Aura has been introduced in India with BS6 compliant petrol and diesel engine options. The vehicle is available in 12 variants and six colour schemes across three engine and two gearbox options. The Aura is the second car in the compact sedan segment and is based on the Grand i10 Nios hatchback.

Hyundai Verna

The Hyundai Verna is available in both petrol and diesel engine options. The sedan is available in four variants - S/S+, SX, SX(O) and SX (O) Turbo variants.The Verna is available in six colour options - phantom black, fiery red, polar white, typhoon silver, titan grey and starry night.

9.28 lakh

Hyundai Santro

Hyundai has brought back the Santro nameplate with a new design and more features. It comes with a petrol engine and a bi-fuel (Petrol with CNG) option. Presently, this car is available in six different variants - Era Executive, Magna Executive, Magna, Sportz Executive, Sportz and Asta.

4.77 lakh
